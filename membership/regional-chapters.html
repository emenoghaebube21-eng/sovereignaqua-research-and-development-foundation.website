OUR NETWORK

Why Regional Chapters Matter

Regional Chapters strengthen the Foundation by encouraging local participation, supporting community engagement, facilitating educational activities, and promoting research collaboration that reflects regional priorities.

Community Engagement

Connect members and stakeholders through local initiatives, educational programmes, and outreach activities.

Collaboration

Encourage partnerships among academic institutions, organizations, businesses, and community groups.

Regional Development

Address local environmental, scientific, and sustainability priorities while contributing to the Foundation's broader mission.

CHAPTER TYPES

Available Chapter Structures

City Chapter

Serves members within a city or metropolitan area.

Regional Chapter

Coordinates activities across multiple communities or administrative regions.

National Chapter

Supports Foundation initiatives throughout a country.

International Chapter

Represents the Foundation in countries outside the principal jurisdiction, subject to applicable laws and Foundation policies.

BENEFITS

Advantages of Becoming a Chapter

  • Participation in Foundation programmes and initiatives.
  • Access to educational and research resources.
  • Networking opportunities with members and partners.
  • Support for local events and outreach activities.
  • Recognition as an official Foundation chapter upon approval.
  • Opportunities to contribute to regional research and community development.
LEADERSHIP

Chapter Leadership Structure

Each Regional Chapter should establish an effective leadership team responsible for governance, member engagement, programme delivery, and communication with the Foundation.

Chapter Chair

Provides strategic leadership, represents the chapter, presides over meetings, and serves as the primary liaison with the Foundation.

Vice Chair

Supports the Chair, coordinates chapter activities, and assumes leadership responsibilities when required.

Secretary

Maintains official records, meeting minutes, membership documentation, and chapter correspondence.

Treasurer

Maintains accurate financial records, prepares reports, and oversees chapter funds in accordance with Foundation policies.

RESPONSIBILITIES

Responsibilities of an Official Chapter

  • Promote the Foundation's mission and values.
  • Recruit and engage members within the chapter's region.
  • Organize educational, research, and community outreach activities.
  • Support environmental stewardship and sustainability initiatives.
  • Maintain transparent governance and financial accountability.
  • Submit reports and updates as requested by the Foundation.
  • Collaborate with other chapters on regional and international programmes.
FORMATION

Requirements for Establishing a Chapter

Founding Members

A chapter should have an appropriate number of committed founding members to support its activities and governance.

Defined Service Area

Each chapter should clearly identify the geographic area or community it intends to serve.

Operating Plan

Applicants should submit an outline describing proposed programmes, objectives, leadership, and planned activities.

APPROVAL

Chapter Recognition Process

Step 1

Submit a Chapter Application with leadership details and proposed service area.

Step 2

Foundation staff review the application for completeness and alignment with organizational policies.

Step 3

Applicants may be asked to provide additional information or participate in an orientation meeting.

Step 4

Approved chapters receive official recognition and onboarding resources.

ACCOUNTABILITY

Reporting & Governance

  • Provide periodic reports on chapter activities and achievements.
  • Maintain accurate membership and administrative records.
  • Comply with Foundation policies, governance standards, and applicable laws.
  • Promote transparency in financial and operational matters.
  • Support continuous improvement through regular evaluation and planning.
FAQ

Regional Chapter Questions

Who can establish a Regional Chapter?

Eligible Foundation members or organizations with the capacity to support local activities may apply, subject to Foundation approval.

Can a chapter organize local events?

Yes. Official chapters are encouraged to organize educational, research, networking, and community engagement activities consistent with Foundation policies.

Does a chapter operate independently?

Chapters manage local activities while remaining accountable to the Foundation's governance framework and policies.

Can chapters collaborate with external organizations?

Yes. Collaboration is encouraged where it aligns with the Foundation's mission, governance requirements, and applicable agreements.
