Integrity in Research, Collaboration & Service

The SovereignAqua Research & Development Foundation is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct, scientific integrity, accountability, professionalism, and respect in every programme, partnership, and research initiative.

INTRODUCTION

Our Ethical Commitment

This Code of Ethics establishes the principles that guide the conduct of all members, volunteers, committee members, institutional representatives, researchers, staff, and partners working with the Foundation. These principles promote integrity, accountability, fairness, professionalism, responsible research, environmental stewardship, respect for human dignity, and compliance with applicable laws and Foundation policies.

OUR VALUES

Core Ethical Values

Integrity

Act honestly, ethically, and transparently in all Foundation activities.

Respect

Treat every individual with dignity, fairness, courtesy, and respect.

Sustainability

Support responsible environmental stewardship and sustainable development.

Collaboration

Encourage teamwork, knowledge sharing, and multidisciplinary cooperation.

Innovation

Promote creativity and responsible scientific advancement.

Global Responsibility

Work toward solutions that benefit communities and the environment worldwide.

ETHICAL PRINCIPLES

Standards of Conduct

Professional Conduct

Members are expected to conduct themselves with honesty, professionalism, accountability, and courtesy in every Foundation activity.

Research Integrity

Members should accurately present research findings, acknowledge sources, avoid plagiarism, and maintain scientific integrity.

Conflict of Interest

Members should disclose actual or potential conflicts of interest that could affect impartial decision-making.

Confidentiality

Confidential information obtained through Foundation activities should be protected and used responsibly.

Respect & Inclusion

Discrimination, harassment, intimidation, and abusive conduct are not consistent with the Foundation's values.

Responsible Resource Use

Foundation property, funding, equipment, and intellectual assets should be used responsibly and only for authorized purposes.

ENVIRONMENTAL STEWARDSHIP

Protecting Our Planet

Environmental responsibility is central to the Foundation's mission. Members are encouraged to support initiatives that conserve natural resources, protect biodiversity, promote responsible water management, and encourage sustainable innovation.

Water Conservation

Support responsible management and protection of freshwater and marine ecosystems.

Environmental Protection

Promote conservation, habitat restoration, and responsible resource use.

Sustainable Practices

Encourage environmentally responsible practices throughout Foundation programmes and operations.

PRIVACY

Confidentiality & Data Protection

Personal Information

Protect personal information entrusted to the Foundation and handle it responsibly in accordance with applicable privacy laws and Foundation policies.

Confidential Information

Safeguard confidential research, organizational records, and proprietary information from unauthorized disclosure.

Digital Security

Use Foundation technology, communication systems, and digital resources responsibly while following established security practices.

REPORTING

Reporting Ethical Concerns

Members are encouraged to report concerns involving unethical conduct, research misconduct, conflicts of interest, fraud, discrimination, harassment, misuse of Foundation resources, or other significant policy violations. Reports should be made in good faith through the Foundation's designated reporting procedures.

Report Concerns

Prompt reporting helps protect the integrity of the Foundation and its programmes.

Review Process

Reported concerns may be reviewed according to Foundation procedures while respecting fairness and confidentiality.

Fair Treatment

Individuals involved in a review should be treated respectfully and afforded an appropriate opportunity to respond.

ACCOUNTABILITY

Consequences of Ethical Violations

  • Educational guidance and corrective action where appropriate.
  • Written warnings for policy violations.
  • Temporary suspension from Foundation activities.
  • Removal from committees or leadership positions.
  • Termination of membership where justified under Foundation procedures.
  • Referral to relevant authorities where required by law.
ETHICS COMMITMENT

Our Shared Pledge

"I commit to upholding the values, principles, and ethical standards of the SovereignAqua Research & Development Foundation. I will conduct myself with integrity, respect, professionalism, accountability, and a commitment to responsible research, collaboration, and sustainable development."
FAQ

Code of Ethics Questions

Who must follow this Code of Ethics?

The Code applies to members, volunteers, researchers, committee members, institutional representatives, staff, and partners participating in Foundation activities.

What should I do if I observe unethical conduct?

Report concerns through the Foundation's designated reporting procedures so they can be reviewed appropriately.

Can ethical violations affect membership?

Yes. Depending on the circumstances and applicable Foundation procedures, violations may result in corrective measures, suspension, or termination of membership.
