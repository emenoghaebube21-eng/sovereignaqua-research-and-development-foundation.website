PURPOSE

Why Committees Matter

Committees strengthen the Foundation by bringing together members with diverse knowledge and experience to advise on programmes, review initiatives, recommend improvements, and support effective governance. Committee recommendations help inform leadership decisions while remaining aligned with the Foundation's mission, governing documents, and applicable policies.

STANDING COMMITTEES

Permanent Committees

Research Committee

Supports research priorities, scientific quality, ethics, and collaborative research initiatives.

Education Committee

Advises on educational programmes, workshops, training, and public awareness initiatives.

Sustainability Committee

Supports environmental stewardship, conservation, and sustainable development projects.

Partnership Committee

Develops relationships with universities, nonprofit organizations, government agencies, and industry partners.

Finance & Resource Committee

Provides oversight and recommendations regarding budgeting, fundraising, grants, and resource stewardship.

Governance & Ethics Committee

Reviews governance practices, ethical standards, and policy recommendations.

SPECIAL COMMITTEES

Project & Advisory Committees

Supports initiatives related to water conservation, aquatic ecosystems, and sustainable water management.

Aquaculture Advisory Group

Provides technical guidance for aquaculture research and sustainable fisheries programmes.

Innovation & Technology Group

Explores emerging technologies and innovative approaches that support the Foundation's objectives.

RESPONSIBILITIES

Committee Responsibilities

  • Review programmes within the committee's area of responsibility.
  • Provide recommendations to Foundation leadership.
  • Support strategic planning and policy development.
  • Encourage collaboration among members and partners.
  • Promote ethical, transparent, and accountable decision-making.
  • Assist in evaluating projects and initiatives where requested.
MEMBERSHIP

Committee Membership

Committee membership is open to qualified Foundation members whose knowledge, experience, and interests align with the committee's responsibilities. Appointments are made according to the Foundation's governance framework and operational needs.

Eligibility

Applicants should maintain active Foundation membership and demonstrate relevant experience, professional conduct, and commitment to the Foundation's mission.

Qualifications

Relevant academic, professional, technical, or community experience may be considered during committee appointments.

Service Commitment

Members are expected to participate actively, attend meetings where possible, and contribute constructively to committee objectives.

LEADERSHIP

Committee Leadership Structure

Committee Chair

Provides leadership, facilitates meetings, coordinates committee activities, and reports recommendations to Foundation leadership.

Vice-Chair

Supports the Chair and assists with coordination, planning, and continuity of committee work.

Secretary

Maintains meeting records, agendas, attendance, and committee documentation.

Committee Members

Participate in discussions, provide expertise, review proposals, and contribute to committee objectives.

MEETINGS

Committee Meeting Procedures

  • Meetings may be held in person, online, or through hybrid formats.
  • Meeting agendas should be distributed before scheduled meetings where practical.
  • Minutes should document key discussions, recommendations, and action items.
  • Committee recommendations should be communicated through established governance channels.
  • Confidential matters should be handled appropriately according to Foundation policies.
APPOINTMENT

How Committee Members Are Appointed

Step 1

Submit an expression of interest or respond to a committee vacancy announcement.

Step 2

Applications or nominations are reviewed according to the committee's needs and Foundation policies.

Step 3

Selected candidates receive appointment confirmation and onboarding information.

Step 4

Committee members begin participating in meetings and assigned responsibilities.

FAQ

Committee Questions

Who may serve on a committee?

Committee positions are generally available to eligible Foundation members whose experience and interests align with the committee's purpose.

Are committee appointments permanent?

Committee appointments are typically made for defined periods or until they are reviewed or concluded under Foundation governance procedures.

Can members serve on more than one committee?

Members may be considered for service on multiple committees when appropriate and when responsibilities can be fulfilled effectively.

How often do committees meet?

Meeting schedules vary according to each committee's responsibilities, current projects, and operational requirements.
